Research Interests

My research lies in nonlinear elliptic and parabolic partial differential equations, with emphasis on fully nonlinear elliptic operators, especially Pucci extremal operators and related non-divergence form equations. I am interested in qualitative questions such as existence, uniqueness, multiplicity, bifurcation, regularity, comparison principles, and the geometric behavior of solutions and eigenvalues.

Fully Nonlinear Elliptic PDEs and Pucci Operators

A central part of my work concerns fully nonlinear elliptic equations involving Pucci extremal operators and related non-divergence form operators. These problems are naturally studied in the framework of viscosity solutions, where maximum principles, comparison arguments, barrier constructions, and principal eigenvalue theory play an important role.

Principal Eigenvalues and Shape Optimization

I am interested in principal eigenvalue problems for fully nonlinear operators and in understanding how the geometry of a domain affects the corresponding eigenvalue. My current work includes shape optimization problems and Pólya-type spectral questions for Pucci-type operators, with particular focus on geometric inequalities, symmetry, and eigenvalue minimization.

Singular Nonlinearities, Multiplicity, and Regularity

Another major direction of my research concerns nonlinear PDEs with singular nonlinearities, natural gradient growth, nonlinear boundary conditions, and coupled systems. I study existence, uniqueness, multiplicity, bifurcation, and regularity questions for such equations and systems, using methods from nonlinear analysis, sub-supersolution techniques, fixed point theory, and viscosity solution methods.

Parabolic Problems and Boundary Behavior

I also work on fully nonlinear parabolic PDEs, particularly questions related to boundary behavior and exceptional boundary sets. This line of work involves the construction of suitable barriers and the study of how geometric or measure-theoretic conditions on boundary sets influence the qualitative behavior of solutions.

Optimal Control and Mathematical Biology

My broader interests include optimal control problems, Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman type equations, and reaction–diffusion models arising in ecology and population dynamics. These problems connect nonlinear PDE theory with models of spatial dispersal, harvesting, grazing, and population interaction.

Current Focus

My current research is focused on shape optimization of principal eigenvalues for fully nonlinear elliptic operators, especially Pucci-type operators, and on qualitative properties of nonlinear PDEs with singular or gradient-dependent terms. I am also interested in collaborative work connecting fully nonlinear PDE theory with geometric analysis, optimal control, and ecological models.
